Strategy: Three Streams, Sequenced

Events remain the flagship

Event 1 sold 94/100 tickets in the debut — the format works. Two more in 2026, spaced roughly two months apart. Rolex-tier prize remains the anchor because it's the proven draw. Save prize variation (Omega, Panerai) for 2027 once we've built a three-event track record.

Native raffle launches after Event 2, not before

Originally planned for June via Raffall — pivoted to native Shopify implementation to keep full margin, full data capture, and full brand control. This slips the raffle launch to August, which is actually better: Event 2 creates the warm audience (100 attendees, press, content, social proof) that the raffle launches off, rather than cold Meta traffic. Event drives raffle drives merch drives Event 3. Flywheel.

Merch drops with events, functions as entry multiplier

Every merch purchase auto-enters the raffle (with free-entry path required for legal compliance). Polos and watch case weighted heavier on entries-per-dollar than tees — rewards higher-intent buyers. Merch doubles as revenue engine and raffle marketing.

Watch Club deferred to Q4/2027

Subscription watch club is validated (Watch Gang proves the mass-market model; Verde's play is premium tier). But don't launch a third stream while two are still unproven. Revisit November after Event 3 revenue lands.

Size distribution mandate. Push the supplier for uneven MOQ per size. Target mix per apparel SKU: S 10% / M 25% / L 35% / XL 20% / XXL 10%. M–L–XL = 80% of sales. Equal-size orders kill first-drop economics.

Event Ticket Tiers — Restructured

Green Jacket retired Reserve name + price open

The original Green Jacket tier ($1,000) transferred ~$1,500 of perceived value to Scoring Club's initiation-waiver funnel — Verde carried the cost of the tier promise without capturing the margin. New structure replaces it with a Verde-controlled premium tier that keeps 100% of value inside the brand and can be sold in higher units without diluting exclusivity.

Tier naming approach. Tier names are event-theme-specific, not brand-static. Event 1 was Masters-themed (Magnolia Lane, Amen Corner, Green Jacket). Each new event's theme writes its own tier names from the same three-tier skeleton. Example: a British Open-themed event might name them The Open, The Claret Jug, The R&A. The price, benefits, and structure of the Premium Tier stay consistent across events — only the naming changes with the theme.
Premium Tier economics. Incremental cost per ticket is ~$25–30 (extra shots are free; watch case is $7; exclusive merch ~$15–20). At $550 revenue per unit, net margin is ~$520 per ticket. Selling 15–20 premium tickets = $7,500–$10,400 in near-pure-margin event revenue that didn't exist at Event 1.

Why the premium tier restructure works

  • Verde keeps the value — no external benefit transferring to Scoring Club's customer funnel
  • Higher unit ceiling — 15–20 units vs 5 Green Jackets means 3–4x more revenue at slightly lower price
  • On-brand benefits — watch case, limited merch, raffle entries all reinforce the Verde et Temps universe (watches + merch + events) instead of pointing customers elsewhere
  • Recurring audience — 2x raffle entries for 12 months and early merch access create ongoing engagement, not one-night value
  • Scalable format — same tier structure and economics repeat across every future event; only the theme-driven name changes

90-Day Content Strategy

Apr 21 – Jul 20 Pre-raffle, pre-Meta-ads window

Event 1 closed on April 11. Event 2 opens August 1 or 8. That four-month silence is the danger zone — if the brand goes quiet between events, the audience forgets and we restart from zero on Event 2 ticket sales. These 90 days are about keeping the brand warm, pre-selling the summer merch drop, building the email list the raffle Meta ads will retarget in August, and converting Event 1 momentum into Event 2 demand.

Strategic anchor. No paid media in this window. Raffle and event Meta ads don't launch until August 8. Everything in these 90 days is organic, email, and earned — which means content quality and consistency carry the entire load.
